# Kawaii Blaster — Game Concept

## What Is It?

Kawaii Blaster is a 2D arcade shooter with a simple, darkly comedic twist: you are supposed to shoot the cute ones.

The game is a parody. It deliberately inverts the moral logic of most shooters — monsters are safe, innocents are the target. The joke is on the player.

## Setting and Look

The game is presented as a first-person view, though no character or hands are visible. The player's only presence on screen is the mouse cursor, which acts as a gun sight.

The visual style is pixelated — chunky sprites, bright colors, the aesthetic of a retro arcade cabinet.

The environment is a single landscape: a horizontal ground line with a little variation each time you play, giving it a slightly different silhouette on every run.

## How It Works

From behind the landscape, sprites pop up briefly and then duck back down. You click to shoot.

**Shoot these — earn points:**
little bunnies, little cats, and other small kawaii creatures. Stylized, adorable, and very much the target.

**Avoid these — or lose health:**
skeletons, zombies, and other monsters. Killing one costs you life energy instead of rewarding you.

The challenge is not reflexes alone — it is resisting the instinct to shoot the monster and instead blasting the bunny.

## The Point

The game has one level. It does not try to be more than it is.

The concept is intentionally absurd: a parody designed to make you feel like a slightly worse person for playing it. In good fun.
